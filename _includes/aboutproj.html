About The Project

This website offers free outreach resources to teach soft robotic principles using rice paper — a rapid, low-cost, and eco-friendly material.

Traditional prototyping often relies on silicone elastomers, which pose environmental challenges due to their use of non-renewable materials, energy-intensive manufacturing, and disposal issues.

Rice paper presents a sustainable alternative, ideal for short-term applications like outreach, prototyping, and culinary or agricultural uses. Here, you'll find:

  • Guidance on how to fabricate soft actuators using rice paper
  • Information on its benefits and end-of-life compostability
  • Ready-to-use outreach resources, including workshop slides and materials lists
  • Mini blog posts on outreach events held using the workshops

Made from natural, plant-based ingredients, these materials offer a renewable, biodegradable option compared to synthetic silicones—helping reduce waste and environmental impact while making robotics accessible and fun.
